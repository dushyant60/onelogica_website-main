Retail Banking Scenarios

1.Customer segmentation: Retail banks can use analytics to segment their customer base based on demographics, transaction history, and other data points, in order to tailor their products and services to specific segments and improve customer retention.

2.Credit risk analysis: Retail banks can use analytics to evaluate the creditworthiness of potential borrowers and assess the risk of default on loans.

3.Fraud detection: Retail banks can use analytics to identify and prevent fraudulent activity by monitoring transactions for unusual patterns or anomalies.

4.Marketing campaign optimization: Retail banks can use analytics to track the effectiveness of their marketing campaigns, and optimize them for better performance.

5.Sales forecasting: Retail banks can use analytics to forecast future sales and adjust their strategies accordingly.

6.Customer lifetime value: Retail banks can use analytics to calculate the expected revenue from a customer over the course of their lifetime, and prioritize efforts to retain high-value customers.

7.Operational efficiency: Retail banks can use analytics to optimize their operations and identify areas where they can improve efficiency and reduce costs.

8.Risk management: Retail banks can use analytics to identify and manage risks across all areas of their business, including credit, market, and operational risks.

9.Predictive maintenance: Retail banks can use analytics to predict equipment failure, schedule maintenance and improve overall efficiency.

10.Chatbot and AI driven customer service: Retail banks can use analytics to train and improve the performance of their chatbot and AI-driven customer service systems.

11.Cross-selling & Up-selling: Retail banks can use analytics to identify products or services that a customer is likely to be interested in, and target them with personalized offers.

12.Churn prediction: Retail banks can use analytics to predict which customers are likely to leave and take proactive steps to retain them.

13.Product profitability: Retail banks can use analytics to determine which products and services are most profitable, and adjust their strategy accordingly.

14.Customer engagement: Retail banks can use analytics to understand how customers interact with their products and services and identify opportunities to improve engagement.

Commercial Banking

1.Portfolio management: Commercial banks can use analytics to manage their loan portfolio and identify opportunities for growth and diversification.

2.Financial forecasting: Commercial banks can use analytics to forecast the financial performance of their clients and make informed lending decisions.

3.Trade finance: Commercial banks can use analytics to monitor the performance of trade finance transactions and identify any potential issues.

4.ALM and Stress testing: Commercial banks can use analytics to perform Asset Liability Management and stress testing to ensure that they can meet the financial obligations.

Banking Lending

1.Collections prediction: Lenders can use analytics to predict which loans are at risk of default and take appropriate action to minimize losses.

2.Affordability assessment: Lenders can use analytics to evaluate a borrower's ability to repay the loan based on their income, expenses, and existing debt.

3.Automated Underwriting: Lenders can use analytics to automate the loan underwriting process, reducing the time and cost of manual review.

4.Behavioural scoring: Lenders can use analytics to assess a borrower's behaviour and predict their likelihood of default.

5.ROI Optimization: Lenders can use analytics to determine the return on investment of different lending products and optimize their strategies accordingly.

Payments

1.Transaction monitoring: Payment providers can use analytics to monitor transactions in real- time and identify any potential issues.

2.Payment behaviour analytics: Payment providers can use analytics to track payment behaviour of customers, such as how often they make payments, their preferred payment methods, and how they respond to failed transactions.

3.Spend analysis: Payment providers can use analytics to track the spending habits of customers, such as the types of merchants they shop at, how much they spend, and when they make purchases.

4.Payment channel optimization: Payment providers can use analytics to understand how customers interact with different payment channels, such as online, mobile, and in-store, and identify opportunities to improve the customer experience.

5.Payment network optimization: Payment providers can use analytics to optimize the performance of their payment network, such as identifying bottlenecks and reducing transaction latency.
